## Schedules

A **schedule** is a rule. When a followed channel goes live, ReplayVOD evaluates every schedule and records the stream if any of them match. Filters available: channel, category, tag, viewer threshold, quality. See [Schedules & title tracking](/docs/schedules/) for the full reference.

## Live detection

ReplayVOD subscribes to Twitch EventSub and acts on `stream.online` deliveries the moment they arrive. EventSub requires a publicly reachable HTTPS callback — direct ([EventSub](/docs/eventsub/)) or via the [Connect relay](/docs/connect/).

## Title tracking

Stream titles and categories often change mid-broadcast. ReplayVOD captures that history while a recording is active. Three modes — see [Schedules & title tracking](/docs/schedules/) for the full table.

## The recording pipeline

Once a stream matches a schedule:

1. Fetch a Twitch playback token (anonymous by default; authenticated if `TWITCH_SERVICE_ACCOUNT_REFRESH_TOKEN` is set).
2. Pull the HLS master playlist; pick a variant per `[download]` policy.
3. Download segments in parallel with retry and gap detection.
4. Remux to MP4 with `ffmpeg`; probe with `ffprobe`.
5. Generate a thumbnail and snapshot strip.
6. Hand the finished file to the storage backend (local move or S3 upload).

Job and segment state lives in the database, so a crash mid-record resumes cleanly on the next start.

## Connect

Twitch EventSub needs a public HTTPS endpoint. Many homelabs don't have one. Connect is a small Cloudflare Worker that:

- Gives you a public ingest URL Twitch posts to.
- Forwards each signed POST to your recorder over an outbound WebSocket.
- Holds no Twitch credentials and never inspects payloads — your recorder still verifies the HMAC locally.

You don't need Connect if you can already expose `/api/v1/webhook/callback` over public HTTPS. See [Connect relay](/docs/connect/) for setup.

<Aside type="note" title="Connect is a strict relay">
  Connect only forwards EventSub webhooks. It does not store recordings, tunnel arbitrary HTTP, or proxy your dashboard.
</Aside>
